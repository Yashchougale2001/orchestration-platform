# IT Policy

All employees must follow IT usage rules, security policies, and device handling protocols.

1. Acceptable Use
Company IT resources are provided for business purposes. Limited personal use is permitted if it does not interfere with work duties.
Employees must not use company systems for illegal activities, harassment, or accessing inappropriate content.
Streaming services and high-bandwidth personal activities should be avoided during business hours.
2. Password & Authentication
Passwords must be at least 12 characters and include uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols.
Passwords must be changed every 90 days and cannot repeat the last 5 passwords.
Multi-factor authentication (MFA) is required for all company systems.
Passwords must never be shared or written down in accessible locations.
3. Device Handling
Company devices must be encrypted and password-protected at all times.
Devices must not be left unattended in public spaces.
Lost or stolen devices must be reported to IT within 1 hour of discovery.
Personal devices connecting to company networks must be approved by IT and comply with security standards (BYOD policy).
4. Data Protection
Confidential data must not be stored on local drives; use approved cloud storage only.
Sensitive files must be encrypted before sharing externally.
USB drives and external storage devices are prohibited unless IT-approved.
Data must be classified according to company data classification guidelines.
5. Email & Communication
Employees must verify sender identity before clicking links or opening attachments.
Suspicious emails must be reported to IT immediately using the phishing report button.
Confidential information must not be sent via unencrypted email.
Auto-forwarding company email to personal accounts is prohibited.
6. Software & Installation
Only IT-approved software may be installed on company devices.
Employees must not disable antivirus, firewalls, or security tools.
All software must be kept up to date; automatic updates must remain enabled.
Unauthorized use of pirated or unlicensed software is strictly prohibited.
7. Network Security
Connecting to unsecured public Wi-Fi without VPN is prohibited.
Employees must use the company VPN when accessing internal systems remotely.
Unauthorized network devices (routers, switches, access points) must not be connected.
8. Remote Work
Remote workspaces must be secure and free from unauthorized viewers.
Screen locks must activate after 5 minutes of inactivity.
Company data must not be accessed from shared or public computers.
Home networks should use WPA3 or WPA2 encryption with strong passwords.
9. Incident Reporting
All security incidents (suspected breaches, malware, unauthorized access) must be reported to IT immediately.
Employees must not attempt to investigate or resolve security incidents independently.
Cooperation with IT during incident investigations is mandatory.
10. Compliance & Enforcement
Violations of this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination.
IT reserves the right to monitor company systems and devices for compliance.
Employees must complete annual IT security awareness training.
